FAQ

1. What is the importance of regular check-ups?
Regular health checks can help to identify early warning signs of disease or illness. Heart disease, diabetes and some cancers can often be picked up in their early stages, when treatment may be more successful.
2. How can I maintain a healthy diet?
Maintaining a healthy diet involves consuming a balanced mix of fruits, vegetables, lean proteins, and whole grains while minimizing processed foods and sugars.
3. What are the common signs of dehydration?
Common signs of dehydration include dry mouth, dark yellow urine, dizziness, and increased thirst. On average, a healthy adult should take in the following amounts per day: 3.7 liters (15.5 cups or 125 ounces) for men. 2.7 liters (11.5 cups or 91 ounces) for women.
4. How often should I exercise for good health?
Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic activity or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity activity per week, along with muscle-strengthening activities on two or more days per week.
5. Why can't I sleep?
In order for our bodies to perform well, providers recommend 7-9 hours of sleep per night. The most common reasons people can not sleep are caffeine, nicotine, or certain medications. Eliminating caffeinated drinks after noon can be beneficial toward your sleep routine.
